The master frame — repeat it on every call

The LLC gives the customer gains — personal liability protection, access to business loans, and significant tax advantages — and a burden: complying with the government at the local, state, and federal level. Everything you sell exists to protect the gains and remove the burden. RA protects what arrives. WFC protects what's due. Tax services protect the money. You are a protector, not a peddler.

The 5 laws of the call

  • Pause after every question. The script literally writes in the pauses. Silence is where customers sell themselves.
  • Ask, don't tell. "Do you see why most people don't list themselves?" beats a lecture. Tie-downs create micro-agreements that compound into the close.
  • Break down every price. $199/year is "less than $1 a day." Never leave a price hanging without a breakdown.
  • Always ask for payment permission. "Do I have your permission to process $___ on the card ending in ___?" Verbatim, every time.
  • Two closes minimum. Every full call attempts RA and WFC. Tax is the third lane when discovery signals it.

02

The Call Control System

lead without pushing

Control isn't dominance — it's structure. The rep who sets the agenda, asks the questions, and summarizes the answers is leading the call, even while talking only 30% of the time.

The 4 control levers

  • 1 · The agenda contract. Their "yes" to "10–15 minutes, sound good?" gives you permission to steer all call. When they drift, you're returning to their agreement, not imposing yours.
  • 2 · Whoever asks the questions, leads. End nearly every statement with a question. If you've made three statements in a row without one, you've lost the wheel.
  • 3 · Summarize to steer. "So what I'm hearing is [their words]…" proves you listened and hands you the right to choose what comes next.
  • 4 · Name the time. "Two quick things left and I'll let you go" creates momentum and reduces resistance to the next pitch.

Redirect lines — the steering wheel

Rambler

"I love that — and it actually ties right into what I wanted to cover next…" validate, then pivot

Tangent

"That's a great question — let me make a note so we hit it before we hang up. First, while I have you…"

Over time

"I promised you 10–15 minutes and I respect your time — do you have a few more so we can finish setting you up right?"

Re-engage

"[Name], let me pause — out of everything we've covered, what matters most to you so far?"

After silence

Say nothing. Count three seconds. The customer fills silence with truth — and often with the close.

A "yes" means advance. A hesitation means stay — you just found the real conversation.

know the why

The scripts aren't magic words — they're applied behavioral science. When you understand the mechanism, you can improvise without breaking the machine.
