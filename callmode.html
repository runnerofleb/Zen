Top 10 Lines to Memorize

word-for-word · week one

These ten lines do 80% of the revenue work. Drill them until they come out of your mouth faster than your own name.

1
The agenda contract
"…and make sure you're prepared and set up for success for this first year and beyond. This should only take about 10–15 minutes — does that sound good?"
Why: their "yes" is a micro-contract. You now own the structure of the call.
2
The credit line
"I know it can feel like a lot upfront, but most people never even get this far… so I just want to give you credit for getting started the right way."
Why: status is a gift. It lands right before the discovery opener on every call — pre-form and post-form.
3
The gains→burden bridge
"With your LLC you gain Personal Liability Protection, access to business loans, and a ton of tax advantages… and at the same time you gain the burden of complying with the government at the local, state, and federal level."
Why: this single frame justifies every product you sell. The master key.
4
The RA intent question
"I see you selected yourself as the Registered Agent for [Business]. I'm curious — did you choose that intentionally, or was that more of a default setup when you formed the LLC?"
Why: zero-pressure opener. "Default setup" is the answer 90% of the time — and it opens the education with their permission.
5
The risk one-liner
"It's one of those things that isn't a big deal… until it is."
Why: loss aversion in nine words. Let it land in silence.
6
The availability fork
"Over the next 12 months, do you expect [yourself / the person listed] to always be available at that address — or is there a chance [you're/they're] away, traveling, working, moving around, or focused on running the business?"
Why: there is no good answer that keeps them as their own RA. The question closes for you.
7
The RA price close
"It's only $199 for the entire year — and after the first year it's still just $199. So if you break it down, you get us to handle this and keep the peace of mind for less than $1 a day."
Why: reframes the anchor from $199 to pocket change, then tie-down: "Does that sound good?"
8
The payment authorization
"Do I have your permission to process the payment of $___ with the card on file ending in [last 4]?"
Why: verbatim, every product, every time. Compliance + confidence in one sentence.
9
The WFC coupon rescue
"So let's do this — let me see if we still have a coupon available for you to add our Annual Worry Free Compliance service for just $149 for your first year. If we can get that discount applied, would that allow you to get this taken care of?"
Why: scarcity + reciprocity — but only after the risk reframe, and only for WFC. Never lead with it. (For tax services, the affordability path is monthly installments — TX §5.)
10
The quarterly hook
"Let's talk about something that catches almost every LLC owner off guard… the IRS expects you to pay taxes on your business profit every few months — not just at tax time."
Why: a genuine surprise creates the information gap that powers the whole tax track.

The master frame — repeat it on every call

The LLC gives the customer gains — personal liability protection, access to business loans, and significant tax advantages — and a burden: complying with the government at the local, state, and federal level. Everything you sell exists to protect the gains and remove the burden. RA protects what arrives. WFC protects what's due. BLR protects the right to open the doors. Tax Solutions and Money Pro protect the money. You are a protector, not a peddler.

The 5 laws of the call

  • Pause after every question. The script literally writes in the pauses. Silence is where customers sell themselves.
  • Ask, don't tell. "Do you see why most people don't list themselves?" beats a lecture. Tie-downs create micro-agreements that compound into the close.
  • Break down every price. $199/year is "less than $1 a day." Never leave a price hanging without a breakdown.
  • Always ask for payment permission. "Do I have your permission to process $___ on the card ending in ___?" Verbatim, every time.
  • Two closes minimum. Every full call attempts RA and WFC. Tax is the third lane when discovery signals it — and EIN, OA, and BLR are 90-second wins hiding in the file.

The Call Control System

lead without pushing

Control isn't dominance — it's structure. The rep who sets the agenda, asks the questions, and summarizes the answers is leading the call, even while talking only 30% of the time.

The 4 control levers

  • 1 · The agenda contract. Their "yes" to "10–15 minutes, sound good?" gives you permission to steer all call. When they drift, you're returning to their agreement, not imposing yours.
  • 2 · Whoever asks the questions, leads. End nearly every statement with a question. If you've made three statements in a row without one, you've lost the wheel.
  • 3 · Summarize to steer. "So what I'm hearing is [their words]…" proves you listened and hands you the right to choose what comes next.
  • 4 · Name the time. "Two quick things left and I'll let you go" creates momentum and reduces resistance to the next pitch.

Redirect lines — the steering wheel

Rambler

"I love that — and it actually ties right into what I wanted to cover next…" validate, then pivot

Tangent

"That's a great question — let me make a note so we hit it before we hang up. First, while I have you…"

Over time

"I promised you 10–15 minutes and I respect your time — do you have a few more so we can finish setting you up right?"

Re-engage

"[Name], let me pause — out of everything we've covered, what matters most to you so far?"

After silence

Say nothing. Count three seconds. The customer fills silence with truth — and often with the close.

A "yes" means advance. A hesitation means stay — you just found the real conversation.

  • Smile before "Hi" — it changes your tone in the first 3 seconds
